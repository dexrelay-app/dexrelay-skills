# Name Score Matrix

Use this matrix to rank finalist names for apps, websites, or shared brands.

Score each dimension from 1 to 5, where:

- 1 = weak
- 3 = acceptable
- 5 = excellent

Apply these weights:

- Problem-solution clarity: 25
- Search intent and keyword fit: 20
- Memorability and brandability: 15
- Competitive distinctiveness: 15
- Domain and handle plausibility: 10
- App Store fit: 10
- Website SEO snippet fit: 5

## Dimension Guidance

### Problem-solution clarity

- 5: immediately communicates what the product helps the user do
- 3: somewhat clear but requires subtitle or tagline support
- 1: vague, abstract, or misleading

### Search intent and keyword fit

- 5: aligns tightly with how target users search or describe the problem
- 3: partial alignment with some missing high-value language
- 1: weak intent match or poor discoverability

### Memorability and brandability

- 5: easy to say, remember, and repeat
- 3: usable but generic or slightly awkward
- 1: forgettable, confusing, or hard to pronounce

### Competitive distinctiveness

- 5: clearly separated from obvious incumbents and category clones
- 3: somewhat differentiated but still near crowded naming patterns
- 1: easily confused with existing products

### Domain and handle plausibility

- 5: short, clean, likely ownable, and low-friction as a URL or handle
- 3: workable with qualifiers or alternate TLDs
- 1: poor domain fit or obvious collision risk

### App Store fit

- 5: fits title and subtitle constraints cleanly and supports ASO strategy
- 3: workable with compromises
- 1: awkward under App Store constraints

### Website SEO snippet fit

- 5: supports a strong meta title, H1, and organic-search framing
- 3: usable but not especially strong
- 1: weak or unnatural in SEO surfaces

## Output Format

Use a table or compact list with:

- Candidate name
- Score by dimension
- Weighted total
- One-line verdict

Example verdict labels:

- Best overall
- Best for ASO
- Best for brand
- Strong backup
- Reject
