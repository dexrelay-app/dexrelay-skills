# Keyword Research Playbook

Use this when the task involves App Store keywords, title/subtitle planning, or website search positioning.

## Inputs

Gather these first when possible:

- product category
- target user
- target market and language
- 3 to 5 seed phrases
- known competitors
- whether the goal is downloads, revenue, activation, or brand clarity

## Expansion Paths

Expand keywords through:

- App Store titles and subtitles from direct competitors
- website headlines and meta titles from adjacent tools
- search-result snippets
- Reddit and community language about the user problem
- synonyms, abbreviations, and job-to-be-done phrasing
- use-case modifiers such as `remote`, `iphone`, `mac`, `build`, `approve`, `deploy`, `session`, `workflow`

## Evaluate Each Candidate

Score or estimate these dimensions:

- relevance to the actual product
- intent to download or sign up
- competition density
- differentiation value
- whether it belongs in title, subtitle, keyword field, or website copy

If no hard search-volume data is available, use a confidence label:

- `Verified`
- `Likely`
- `Speculative`

## Strategic Buckets

Group output into:

- **Primary keywords**: highest-value core terms that define the product
- **Secondary keywords**: supporting terms that widen coverage
- **Long-tail phrases**: specific, lower-competition, or audience-shaped language
- **Protected differentiators**: phrases competitors are weak at or not claiming clearly

## App Store Placement Rules

- title: 1 or 2 highest-priority concepts only
- subtitle: adjacent benefits or modifiers, no title repetition
- keyword field: fill remaining territory without repeating title/subtitle words
- description: conversion-first, but still reinforce the same positioning language

## Website Placement Rules

- H1: strongest human-readable promise
- meta title: intent-rich version of the promise
- meta description: outcome plus trust or privacy angle
